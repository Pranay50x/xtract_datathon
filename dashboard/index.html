Strategic Recommendations

Reading Journey Displays

Create physical and digital displays that showcase common reading paths, helping patrons discover related materials. For example, a "From Programming to Mathematics" display could guide Computer Science students toward relevant mathematical texts.

Implementation:

  • Identify 3-5 common reading journeys from data
  • Create visual pathway displays with featured books
  • Include QR codes linking to full reading lists

Schedule-Optimized Programming

Align events and workshops with documented patron activity patterns. Host technical workshops during peak Computer Science borrowing periods and position interdisciplinary events during times when diverse reading is more common.

Implementation:

  • Programming workshops in January-February (peak CS interest)
  • Ethics/professional development sessions in March-April
  • Schedule events Tuesday-Thursday when activity is highest

Department-Specific Outreach

Develop tailored services and collections for departments based on their unique reading profiles. Target CSE and ME departments for interdisciplinary collection development given their diverse reading patterns.

Implementation:

  • Create department-specific reading lists based on historical patterns
  • Host department reading showcases featuring unexpected interests
  • Partner with departments showing high reading diversity for special collections

Collection Development Strategy

Refine acquisition policies based on utilization patterns and reading journeys. Focus on strengthening identified pathways and addressing underutilized subjects through targeted promotion or adjustment.

Implementation:

  • Increase holdings in high-transition areas (CS→Math, Engineering→Physics)
  • Review underutilized collections for currency and appeal
  • Develop bridge materials for common subject transitions
